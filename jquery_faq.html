Info about National Parks

#1
There are 63 National Parks in the US.
#2
The Unique parks to go to are: Grand Canyon, Yosemite, Yellowstone, Rocky Mountains, Zion, Arches, Acadia, Great Smokey Mountains and Everglades
#3
The most overnight stayed parks are: Grand Canyon, Yosemite and Yellowstone
#4
The parks with most amount of wildlife are: Theodore Roosevelt, Isle Royale, Rocky Mountains, Alasks's Denali
#5
Each Park have the ability to have Guided Tours but must visit the visitor and welcome centers.
#6
Last year there were 327 million park visits in the US.
#7
Each Park has its own specific rules which can be found at their local visitor centers.
#8
Each park has digital and downloadable maps as well as hard copies.
#9
Campsites are available on a first-come first serve basis but you can still make reservations online.
#10
National Parks were established by Congress in 1916 to give American people the opportunity to enjoy public lands and make recreational activity more accessible to the public.

Zion

  • Zion Angels landing provides great views but its also one the world's most dangerous.
  • Zion Canyon is continually changing.
  • Zion is a climbers paradise.
  • The park has a Subway, but it's not a form of public transportation.

Yosemite

  • Yosemite's granite rock formations glow like fire at sunset.
  • Is home to one of the tallest waterfalls in the world at 2425 feet.
  • Is one of the few places in the US where you can see a rainbow at night.
  • Is the only national park to bid to host the winter Olympics.

Everglades

  • Is home to one of the largest wetlands in the world.
  • Receives an average of 60 inches of rain a year.
  • Contains the largest contiguous stand of protected mangroves in the western hemisphere.
  • A Nike Hercules Missile Base is still housed in the heart of the Everglades
